There is nothing quite like the silence of the desert at 4:30 in the morning.

When I packed my bags for this trip, the goal was simple: document the sprawling landscapes of the American Southwest with a focus on minimalism and shadow. The reality, of course, involved a lot more coffee, flat tires, and missed alarms.

### The Planning Phase

Preparation is everything when you're shooting landscapes. The light waits for no one. I spent weeks pouring over topographic maps and weather apps, trying to pinpoint the exact locations where the morning sun would hit the canyon walls at just the right angle.

> "Light makes photography. Embrace light. Admire it. Love it. But above all, know light." — George Eastman

### Gear Breakdown

For this trip, I decided to pack relatively light:
*   **Sony A7IV** - The dynamic range is absolutely essential for these high-contrast desert scenes.
*   **24-70mm f/2.8 GM** - My workhorse. 80% of the shots on this trip were taken with this lens.
*   **100-400mm GM** - Crucial for isolating specific patterns in the rock formations from miles away.
